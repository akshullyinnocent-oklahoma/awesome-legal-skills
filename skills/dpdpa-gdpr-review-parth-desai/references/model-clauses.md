# Model Clauses — DPDPA & GDPR Compliant Language

Ready-to-use replacement clauses. Adapt party names / jurisdictions as needed.
Always recommend legal counsel review before final adoption.

---

## 1. Consent & Purpose (Dual-Compliant)

### 1A. Consent Notice (DPDPA §6 / GDPR Art. 13)

> **Data Collection Notice**
>
> [Company Name] ("Data Fiduciary" / "Controller") collects the following personal data from you:
> [list categories].
>
> **Purpose:** [specific, clearly stated purpose only].
>
> **Legal basis (GDPR):** [Consent / Contract performance / Legitimate interests — state which].
>
> You have the right to access, correct, erase, and port your data, and to withdraw consent at any time
> by [specific mechanism, e.g., clicking 'Unsubscribe' / emailing privacy@company.com].
> Withdrawal will not affect processing carried out before withdrawal.
>
> For complaints, contact our Grievance Officer / DPO: [name, email, address].
> You may also lodge a complaint with [DPBI / your local supervisory authority].

## 2. Consent Withdrawal (DPDPA §6(7) / GDPR Art. 7(3))

> You may withdraw your consent to any or all processing at any time by [specific method].
> Withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
> We will act on your withdrawal within [X] business days. Certain processing necessary for
> [legal obligation / contract performance] may continue after withdrawal on a different lawful basis,
> which we will notify you of.

## 3. Data Retention & Erasure (DPDPA §8(7) / GDPR Art. 5(1)(e), Art. 17)

> We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this
> [Policy / Agreement], or as required by applicable law.
>
> | Data Category | Retention Period | Basis for Retention |
> |---------------|-----------------|---------------------|
> | [Account data] | [Duration of account + X years] | [Contract / Legal obligation] |
> | [Transaction records] | [X years from transaction date] | [Tax law / Regulatory requirement] |
> | [Marketing data] | [Until consent withdrawn] | [Consent] |
>
> Upon expiry of the applicable retention period, or upon verified request for erasure, we will
> securely delete or anonymise the data within [X] days, subject to any overriding legal obligation.

## 4. Data Processor Agreement Clause (DPDPA §8(2) / GDPR Art. 28)

> **Data Processing Terms**
>
> Where [Processor Name] processes personal data on behalf of [Controller Name], Processor shall:
> (a) process personal data only on documented instructions from Controller;
> (b) ensure that persons authorised to process data are bound by confidentiality;
> (c) implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures (see Schedule [X]);
> (d) not engage any sub-processor without prior specific or general written authorisation of Controller,
>     and impose equivalent obligations on any sub-processor;
> (e) assist Controller in responding to data subject rights requests;
> (f) assist Controller with obligations under Art. 32–36 GDPR / §8(5)–(6) DPDPA as applicable;
> (g) at Controller's election, delete or return all personal data upon termination of services;
> (h) make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow audits.

## 5. Cross-Border Transfer (DPDPA §16 / GDPR Art. 46)

### For GDPR (EU → Third Country)
> Personal data may be transferred to, and processed in, countries outside the European Economic Area.
> Such transfers occur only:
> (a) to countries subject to an adequacy decision by the European Commission; or
> (b) subject to Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission (2021/914/EU),
>     a copy of which is available on request; or
> (c) pursuant to Binding Corporate Rules approved by the competent supervisory authority.

### For DPDPA (India → Outside India)
> Personal data shall not be transferred outside India except to countries notified by the Central
> Government as permissible destinations under §16 of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023.
> The current list of permissible countries is available at [URL / on request].

---

## 6. Breach Notification (DPDPA §8(6) / GDPR Art. 33–34)

> In the event of a personal data breach, [Party] shall:
> (a) notify [the Data Protection Board of India / the competent supervisory authority] without undue
>     delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach;
> (b) notify affected Data Principals / Data Subjects without undue delay where the breach is likely
>     to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms;
> (c) document all breaches, including those not notifying a supervisory authority, with facts,
>     effects, and remedial action taken.
>
> Notifications shall include: (i) nature of the breach; (ii) categories and approximate number of
> data subjects and records affected; (iii) contact details of DPO / Grievance Officer; (iv) likely
> consequences; (v) measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

## 7. Children's Data (DPDPA §9 / GDPR Art. 8)

> [Company] does not knowingly collect or process personal data of children under [18 / 16 / 13 —
> specify applicable threshold]. Where a user's age cannot be verified and is suspected to be below
> the applicable threshold, processing shall cease immediately pending verifiable parental or guardian
> consent.
>
> We shall not process children's data for behavioural monitoring, targeted advertising, or any
> purpose that may be detrimental to the child's well-being.
>
> Parental/guardian consent shall be obtained through [describe verifiable mechanism].

## 8. Data Subject / Principal Rights Mechanism

> **Exercising Your Rights**
>
> You may exercise your rights under [DPDPA 2023 / GDPR] at any time by contacting us at:
> **Email:** [privacy@company.com]
> **Portal:** [URL]
>
> We will respond within **[30 days / one month]** of receipt of a verified request. This period may
> be extended by a further **[30 days / two months]** where requests are complex or numerous, with
> notice to you within the first [30-day / one-month] period.
>
> We may request additional information to verify your identity before processing a rights request.
> We will not charge a fee except where requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive.

## 9. Legitimate Interests Balancing Recital (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f))

> We rely on legitimate interests as the lawful basis for [specific processing activity]. Our
> legitimate interest is [specific interest]. We have conducted a balancing test and determined that
> our interests are not overridden by your interests, rights, or freedoms because [brief reasoning:
> e.g., the processing is limited in scope, you would reasonably expect it, minimal privacy impact].
> You have the right to object to this processing at any time; see Section [X].

> ⚠️ **Note to drafter:** Document the full Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) internally.
> This recital provides notice only; the LIA must be retained as evidence of accountability (Art. 5(2)).
