## 6. Practical Drafting Tips

### Good wording
- "Article 27 appears applicable because the deployer is a public-law body and intends to use a high-risk AI system in a specific administrative process."
- "The main rights risk is not only data protection, but also equal treatment and effective remedy."
- "Human oversight exists formally, but in its current design may not sufficiently counter automation bias."
- "Residual risk remains high for vulnerable claimants unless the review and complaint process is strengthened before deployment."

### Weak wording to avoid
- "All necessary measures are in place."
- "No fundamental rights are affected."
- "The DPIA covers everything already."
- "Notification is probably unnecessary" without reasons.

## Final Reminder

A strong FRIA deliverable is concrete, use-case-specific, and candid about uncertainty. The document should help the deployer make a real decision - not merely create paper comfort.
