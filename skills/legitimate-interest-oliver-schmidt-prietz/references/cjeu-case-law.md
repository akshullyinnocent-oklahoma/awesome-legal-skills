# CJEU Case Law Quick Reference — Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR

## Key Cases by Theme

### Foundational / Three-Step Test

**C-252/21 Meta v. Bundeskartellamt (4 July 2023)**
- Three cumulative conditions confirmed (para. 106)
- Legal bases must be interpreted **restrictively** (paras. 92-93)
- Necessity: less intrusive alternatives test (para. 108)
- Link to data minimisation principle (para. 109)
- Users of free social network cannot reasonably expect off-platform tracking for personalised advertising, even though service is ad-funded (para. 117)
- Product improvement interest "cannot be ruled out from the outset" (para. 122)
- Law enforcement interest is NOT a legitimate interest for a commercially oriented controller (para. 124)
- Continuous monitoring creates feeling of constant observation (para. 118)
- Data set containing even one sensitive item → entire set is deemed sensitive (para. 89)
- Sensitive data inference: objectively possible = sufficient, regardless of intent (para. 69)
- Scale of processing relevant in balancing (para. 116)

**C-26/22 & C-64/22 SCHUFA Holding (7 December 2023)**
- Three-step test reaffirmed (para. 75)
- Processing must be "in so far as strictly necessary" (para. 88)
- Creditworthiness assessment can be legitimate interest (para. 83)
- Controller and third party can have simultaneous legitimate interests (para. 83)
- Steps 2 and 3 may merge in some cases (para. 92)
- Art. 21 right to object: controller must demonstrate overriding compelling legitimate grounds (para. 111)
- Successful objection → data subject entitled to erasure under Art. 17(1)(c) (paras. 111-112)

**C-708/18 Asociaţia de Proprietari (11 December 2019)**
- Three cumulative conditions (para. 40)
- Interest must be "present and effective at the date of the data processing" — not hypothetical (para. 44)
- Data minimisation and necessity linked (para. 48)
- Publicly available data does NOT automatically allow processing (para. 54)
- Reasonable expectations based on relationship (para. 58)

### Commercial Interests

**C-621/22 KNLTB (4 October 2024)**
- **Commercial interests CAN constitute legitimate interest**, provided not contrary to law (paras. 40, 49)
- Must be assessed on a case-by-case basis
- Tennis association sharing member data with gambling sponsor
- Must consider harmful downstream effects (gaming addiction risk) (para. 56)
- Reasonable expectations of members relevant (para. 55)
- Data minimisation applies with full force (paras. 42-43, 51-52)

**C-17/22 & C-18/22 HTB Neunte Immobilien Portfolio (12 September 2024)**
- Investment fund partner obtaining data about other partners for negotiations — potentially legitimate third-party interest (paras. 56-57)
- Contractual provisions may influence reasonable expectations (para. 64)
- Case-by-case assessment required, considering legal framework and circumstances

### Necessity / Proportionality

**C-524/06 Huber (16 December 2008)**
- "Necessity" has independent meaning in EU law (para. 52)

**C-13/16 Rīgas satiksme (4 May 2017)**
- Third party's interest in obtaining identity of person who caused property damage = legitimate (para. 29)
- Balancing must be performed for each processing operation (para. 28)
- Even manifestly public data does not automatically allow processing (para. 32)

### No Hierarchy of Legal Bases

**C-394/23 Mousse (9 January 2025)**
- No hierarchy among the six legal bases in Art. 6(1) (AG Szpunar Opinion, paras. 28-29)

### Sensitive Data

**C-252/21 Meta (repeated)**
- The relevant question: is it **objectively possible** to infer sensitive information from processed data? (para. 69)
- Irrelevant whether controller intends to extract sensitive information (para. 69)
- Data set with at least one sensitive item → deemed sensitive in entirety (para. 89)

### Automated Decisions / Scoring

**C-634/21 SCHUFA Holding — Scoring (7 December 2023)**
- Art. 22 prohibition is a general rule, not requiring individual invocation (para. 52)
- Member States cannot definitively prescribe the balancing result through national law (para. 70)
- Art. 6(1)(f) is NOT "Union law" authorising automated decisions under Art. 22(2)(b)

### Right to Object / Erasure

**C-26/22 SCHUFA (repeated)**
- "Compelling legitimate grounds" = higher threshold than initial LIA (para. 111)
- Must be essential, not merely beneficial
- Successful objection triggers right to erasure (Art. 17(1)(c)) (paras. 111-112)

**C-131/12 Google Spain (13 May 2014)**
- Right to erasure/de-listing against search engines
- Particular situation of data subject must be assessed (para. 76)
- Access to information online = legitimate interest (para. 81)

### Other Relevant Cases

**C-582/14 Breyer (19 October 2016)**
- Website operator's interest in continued functioning = legitimate
- IP address logging for security purposes

**C-597/19 M.I.C.M. (17 June 2021)**
- Copyright holder's interest in obtaining IP addresses of file sharers for legal claims = legitimate (para. 108)

**C-468/10 & C-469/10 ASNEF (24 November 2011)**
- Publicly available data does not automatically override data subject interests (para. 44)
- Member States cannot add extra conditions to Art. 6(1)(f) that would categorically exclude certain categories of data (pre-GDPR, but principle remains)

**C-434/16 Nowak (20 December 2017)**
- Accuracy assessed in light of processing purpose (para. 53)
- Right to rectification cannot be used to change subjective evaluations (para. 52)

## Quick Citation Reference

When citing CJEU cases in LIA documentation, use format:

> CJEU, judgment of [date], Case [number], [short name] (ECLI:[identifier]), para. [X].

Example:
> CJEU, judgment of 4 July 2023, Case C-252/21, Meta v. Bundeskartellamt (ECLI:EU:C:2023:537), para. 106.
