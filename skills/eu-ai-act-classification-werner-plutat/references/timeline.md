## 1. Key Dates Overview

| Date | What Applies | Provisional Digital Omnibus (if adopted) |
|------|-------------|------------------------------------------|
| **2 Feb 2025** | Prohibited practices ban (Art. 5) + AI literacy obligation (Art. 4) | Would expand the prohibited-practices list with CSAM and non-consensual intimate content; compliance by 2 Dec 2026 |
| **2 Aug 2025** | GPAI model obligations (Arts. 51-56) + governance & penalties framework | Unchanged |
| **2 Aug 2026** | High-risk AI system obligations (Annex III systems) | Would shift to **2 Dec 2027** |
| **2 Aug 2026** | Article 50 transparency rules + Commission GPAI enforcement powers | Art. 50 watermarking would shift to 2 Dec 2026; GPAI enforcement unchanged |
| **2 Aug 2027** | High-risk obligations for Annex I regulated product AI + legacy GPAI | Annex I would shift to **2 Aug 2028**; legacy GPAI unchanged |

**Entry into force:** 1 August 2024
**Full application:** 2 August 2027 (staggered as above; provisional agreement would extend to 2 August 2028 if adopted)

**Status of the Digital Omnibus (as of 2026-05-08):** The European Commission proposed the Digital Omnibus simplification package in December 2025. On 7 May 2026, the Council and Parliament reached a provisional political agreement on the AI Act amendments. The agreement is **not yet adopted law** — it must still be formally adopted by Council and Parliament and published in the Official Journal. Until then, the **current-law dates** above remain authoritative for compliance advice. Communicate the Provisional column only with explicit "if adopted" framing.

**Before advising on urgency:** Always compare the current date against these milestones. Do not assume which deadlines have passed or are upcoming without checking. Do not communicate the provisional dates as the binding deadline.

## 2. Phase 1: Prohibited Practices + AI Literacy (2 February 2025)

### Prohibited Practices (Article 5)

All eight prohibited practices are banned. Systems falling under Article 5 must be:
- Taken off the market
- Taken out of service
- Remediated or discontinued

**No transition period.** Violations risk fines up to €35M or 7% of global annual turnover.

**Immediate action items:**
- [ ] Complete Article 5 assessment for all AI systems in scope
- [ ] Identify and remediate any prohibited systems
- [ ] Document assessment results and remediation steps
- [ ] Escalate borderline cases to legal counsel

### AI Literacy (Article 4)

Providers and deployers must ensure sufficient AI literacy among staff involved in AI system operation and use.

**Immediate action items:**
- [ ] Identify staff involved in AI system operation and use
- [ ] Develop or procure AI literacy training appropriate to the context
- [ ] Roll out training and document completion
- [ ] Establish ongoing refresher cycle

## 3. Phase 2: GPAI Model Obligations + Governance (2 August 2025)

### GPAI Model Obligations (Articles 51–56)

All GPAI model providers must comply with:
- Technical documentation requirements
- Information to downstream providers
- Training data summary (publicly available)
- Copyright compliance policy

**Systemic risk GPAI models** additionally:
- Model evaluations and adversarial testing
- Incident reporting to AI Office
- Cybersecurity measures
- Risk mitigation

**Action items by role:**

*If you are a GPAI model provider:*
- [ ] Prepare Article 53 technical documentation
- [ ] Publish training data summary
- [ ] Establish copyright opt-out compliance process
- [ ] Assess whether your model meets systemic risk thresholds
- [ ] If systemic risk: implement evaluation, testing, and incident reporting

*If you use GPAI models (downstream provider/deployer):*
- [ ] Identify all GPAI models in your supply chain
- [ ] Request Article 53 documentation from model providers
- [ ] Review training data summaries
- [ ] Update vendor contracts with AI Act compliance clauses
- [ ] Assess ability to meet your own obligations with available GPAI documentation

### Governance Framework

- EU AI Office operational
- National competent authorities designated
- Market surveillance structures established
- Penalty framework in effect

**Note for existing GPAI models:** Models placed on the market before 2 August 2025 must comply by **2 August 2027**.

## 4. Phase 3: High-Risk Annex III — 2 August 2026

### High-Risk System Obligations for Annex III Use Cases

Full Title III requirements apply to high-risk AI systems under Annex III:
- Risk management system (Art. 9)
- Data governance (Art. 10)
- Technical documentation (Art. 11)
- Logging (Art. 12)
- Transparency (Art. 13)
- Human oversight (Art. 14)
- Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity (Art. 15)
- Quality management system (Art. 17)
- Conformity assessment (Arts. 43–46)
- CE marking and registration (Arts. 47–49)
- Post-market monitoring (Art. 72)
- Incident reporting (Art. 73)

**Action items (start 12+ months before):**

*Providers:*
- [ ] Complete AI system inventory and classification
- [ ] Establish risk management system for each high-risk system
- [ ] Implement data governance and quality framework
- [ ] Prepare technical documentation
- [ ] Design and implement logging capabilities
- [ ] Create instructions for use (Art. 13)
- [ ] Implement human oversight mechanisms
- [ ] Conduct accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity testing
- [ ] Establish quality management system
- [ ] Plan conformity assessment (self-assessment or notified body)
- [ ] Prepare EU declaration of conformity
- [ ] Register in EU database
- [ ] Set up post-market monitoring programme
- [ ] Establish incident reporting procedures

*Deployers:*
- [ ] Identify all high-risk AI systems in use
- [ ] Develop operational procedures per provider instructions
- [ ] Train human oversight personnel
- [ ] Implement logging and monitoring
- [ ] Conduct FRIA where required (Art. 27)
- [ ] Notify affected workers (Art. 26(7))
- [ ] Register as deployer in EU database where required
- [ ] Establish incident escalation procedures

## 5. Phase 4: High-Risk Annex I — 2 August 2027

### High-Risk Obligations for Regulated Product AI

Same Title III requirements as Phase 3, but for AI systems that are products or safety components under EU harmonisation legislation in Annex I (medical devices, machinery, aviation, automotive, etc.).

**These systems must integrate AI Act requirements with existing product regulation:**
- Conformity assessment may be combined with existing product certification
- Notified bodies need AI Act competency
- Technical documentation must cover both product and AI requirements

**Action items (start 18+ months before):**
- [ ] Map AI components in regulated products
- [ ] Assess conformity assessment route overlap
- [ ] Coordinate with notified bodies on combined assessment
- [ ] Prepare integrated technical documentation

## 6. Practical Planning Guide

### Planning Framework

Use the current date to decide which actions are overdue, urgent, or future-facing. Do not hardcode relative countdowns; calculate urgency from the canonical dates in Section 1.

**Already applicable / baseline controls:**
- Prohibited practices screening for all AI systems
- AI literacy programme launched
- Executive awareness briefing on penalties

**Inventory and classification foundation:**
- GPAI vendor due diligence and contract updates
- Full AI system inventory completed
- Classification of all systems (prohibited / high-risk / limited / minimal)
- Assign compliance ownership per system

**High-risk implementation buildout:**
- Begin high-risk compliance implementation for Annex III systems
- Risk management systems designed
- Data governance frameworks established
- Human oversight procedures drafted

**Evidence and assessment readiness:**
- Technical documentation complete
- Conformity assessment preparations
- Quality management system operational
- Post-market monitoring framework ready

**Final readiness before the applicable deadline:**
- Conformity assessment completed
- EU declaration of conformity prepared
- CE marking ready
- Registration in EU database
- All deployer procedures operational

### Resource Estimation (Rules of Thumb)

| Activity | Typical effort (per system) |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Classification assessment | 2–5 days |
| Risk management system | 15–30 days |
| Technical documentation | 10–20 days |
| Conformity assessment preparation | 10–30 days |
| QMS establishment (organisation-wide) | 30–60 days |
| FRIA (per system/use case) | 3–5 days |
| AI literacy programme (organisation-wide) | 5–15 days |

## 7. Compliance Roadmap Template

Use this structure for project planning:

```
Phase 0: Awareness & Inventory (NOW)
├── Executive briefing on AI Act obligations and penalties
├── Complete AI system inventory
├── Article 5 prohibited practices screening (OVERDUE)
├── AI literacy programme launch
└── GPAI model identification and vendor outreach

Phase 1: Classification & Gap Analysis (3 months)
├── Classify all systems (prohibited / high-risk / limited / minimal / GPAI)
├── Identify role per system (provider / deployer / importer)
├── Gap analysis against applicable requirements
├── Assign compliance ownership and budget
└── Establish AI governance committee

Phase 2: Implementation (6–12 months)
├── Risk management system per high-risk system
├── Data governance and quality frameworks
├── Technical documentation
├── Logging and monitoring implementation
├── Human oversight mechanisms
├── Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity testing
├── QMS establishment
├── FRIA completion (deployers)
└── Vendor contract updates (GPAI + supply chain)

Phase 3: Verification & Registration (3–6 months before deadline)
├── Internal conformity assessment dry run
├── Formal conformity assessment
├── EU declaration of conformity
├── CE marking
├── EU database registration
├── Post-market monitoring operational
└── Incident reporting procedures tested

Phase 4: Ongoing Compliance
├── Post-market monitoring and trend analysis
├── Periodic conformity re-assessment
├── Incident response and reporting
├── Regulatory update tracking (delegated acts, guidance)
├── AI literacy refresher training
└── Annual AI Act compliance review
```
