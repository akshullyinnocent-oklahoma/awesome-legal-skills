## Status within this skill — CORE JUDGMENT for functional/administrative texts

> **Important scope delimitation**: Funke Medien is overall an important Grand Chamber judgment addressing **three** preliminary questions:
>
> 1. Full or partial harmonisation of art. 2(a), 3(1) and 5(2)/(3) Directive 2001/29 (paragraphs 27-54)
> 2. Balancing of interests in applying exceptions (paragraphs 65-76)
> 3. Can fundamental rights justify extra-directive exceptions? (paragraphs 55-64)
>
> These three questions fall **outside** the scope of this skill (work concept + originality) and belong in a separate skill on copyright exceptions and the communication right.
>
> Funke Medien is for this skill a **core judgment** because of the **preliminary observations** (paragraphs 16-26), in which the Court expressly applies the two-step test to functional/administrative texts — specifically military status reports ("Afghanistan Papers"). It is the **first CJEU application** of the Levola+Cofemel consolidation to purely informative texts, and gives operational substance to the idea/expression merger for functional texts.

## Subject matter and category

- **Main category for this skill**: **functional/administrative texts** — application of the two-step test to reports, status reports, military briefings, technical texts
- **Special contribution**:
  - **Confirmation of the cumulative two-step test** for texts (paragraph 19) ⭐
  - **Infopaq formula** applied to textual works: "choice, sequence and combination of words" (paragraph 23)
  - **Operational formula for idea/expression merger** in functional texts (paragraph 24): when "the information and the expression of those reports become indissociable" and the text has "a purely technical function", that excludes originality ⭐⭐
  - **Per-case assessment** by the national court (paragraphs 22, 25): there is no categorical exclusion of administrative texts, nor automatic protection
  - **Synthesis of earlier case-law**: Levola Hengelo (work concept), Painer (free creative choices), Infopaq (choice/sequence/combination), BSA (technical function), SAS Institute (idea/expression), Football Dataco (sweat of the brow excluded) — all expressly cross-referenced
- **Applicable legislation (for this skill)**: Directive 2001/29/EC, art. 2(a) and art. 3(1)
- **Relevance for the skill**: ★★★★★ — *the definitive substantiation of the two-step test for purely factual and administrative texts; fills the gap in the sector module 06-text-and-fragments*

## Facts in four lines

The German Bundeswehr weekly draws up status reports on foreign interventions, the "Unterrichtung des Parlaments" (UdP), classified as "restricted distribution". Funke Medien — publisher of the daily Westdeutsche Allgemeine Zeitung — obtained access to a large part of those UdPs and published them as "Afghanistan-Papiere" on its website, with introductory notes, links and comment function. The Federal Republic of Germany sued for copyright infringement and obtained an injunction at first instance and on appeal. The German court had assumed in those decisions that the UdPs were protected as "literary works" — without however identifying the concrete original features — whereupon the Bundesgerichtshof referred the case on Revision to the Court.

## Key reasoning relevant for this skill (paragraphs 16-26)

### Paragraph 18 — *work as a condition for protection*

> "[S]ubject matter can be protected by copyright under Directive 2001/29 only if such subject matter can be classified as a 'work' within the meaning of those provisions."

Cross-reference Levola Hengelo: the work concept is the gateway threshold.

### Paragraph 19 — *the cumulative two-step test confirmed* ⭐

> "[I]n order for subject matter to be regarded as a 'work', two conditions must be satisfied cumulatively. First, the subject matter must be original in the sense that it is its author's own intellectual creation. In order for an intellectual creation to be regarded as an author's own it must reflect the author's personality, which is the case if the author was able to express his creative abilities in the production of the work by making free and creative choices."

Cross-reference Painer paragraphs 87-89: the triple formulation (author's own intellectual creation + personality + free creative choices).

### Paragraph 20 — *only expression elements qualify*

> "[O]nly something which is the expression of the author's own intellectual creation may be classified as a 'work' within the meaning of Directive 2001/29."

Levola confirmation: step 2 is "expression of the intellectual creation".

### Paragraph 22 — *national court determines per case*

> "It is for the national court to determine whether military status reports, such as those at issue in the main proceedings, **or certain elements thereof, may be regarded as 'works'** within the meaning of Article 2(a) and of Article 3(1) of Directive 2001/29 and therefore be protected by copyright."

### Paragraph 23 — *Infopaq formula applied to textual works* ⭐

> "[T]he originality of which arises from the choice, sequence and combination of the words by which the author expressed his or her creativity in an original manner and achieved a result which is an intellectual creation, whereas **the mere intellectual effort and skill of creating those reports are not relevant in that regard**."

Two important elements:
- **Infopaq triad**: choice, sequence, combination of words — the core test for textual works
- **Sweat of the brow excluded** (cross-reference Football Dataco paragraph 33): intellectual effort and skill are not relevant

### Paragraph 24 — *idea/expression merger for purely informative documents* ⭐⭐⭐ CORE

> "If military status reports, such as those at issue in the main proceedings, constitute purely informative documents, **the content of which is essentially determined by the information which they contain, so that such information and the expression of those reports become indissociable and that those reports are thus entirely characterised by their technical function, precluding all originality**, it should be considered [...] that, in drafting those reports, **it was impossible for the author to express his or her creativity in an original manner and to achieve a result which is that author's own intellectual creation**."

This is the **operational core test for functional texts**:

1. **Purely informative character**: content entirely determined by the information
2. **Idea/expression merger**: information and expression coincide
3. **Purely technical function**: text serves only information transfer
4. **Consequence**: originality excluded → no work

Cross-references:
- **BSA paragraphs 48-50**: technical-function exclusion (originally for GUIs, here generalised)
- **SAS Institute paragraph 67**: anti-monopolisation; idea/expression dichotomy

### Paragraph 25 — *operational summary for military reports*

> "[M]ilitary status reports, such as those at issue in the main proceedings, can be protected by copyright only if those reports are an intellectual creation of their author which reflect the author's personality and are expressed by free and creative choices made by that author in drafting those reports, **which must be ascertained by the national court in each case**."

No categorical exclusion of administrative texts; no automatic protection. Per-case assessment with the two-step formula.

## Doctrinal significance for this skill

### 1. First express CJEU application to functional texts

Preceding judgments concerned specific categories of work:
- Infopaq → press articles
- Painer → portrait photograph
- BSA → GUI
- FAPL → sports matches
- Football Dataco → fixture-list database
- SAS Institute → software
- Levola → taste
- Cofemel → clothing model
- Brompton → physical useful article
- Renckhoff → travel photograph (confirmation)

Funke Medien is the **first time** the CJEU expressly applies the two-step test to **purely factual / administrative / functional texts**. That fills an important gap for practice.

### 2. Synthesis judgment

The judgment contains in five paragraphs (18-25) a complete synthesis of EU doctrine:

| Aspect | Cross-reference |
|---|---|
| Work-concept requirement | Levola Hengelo paragraph 34 |
| Cumulative two-step test | Painer paragraphs 87-89 |
| Expression element | Levola Hengelo paragraph 37 |
| Per-case assessment | Infopaq International paragraph 48 |
| Originality via choice/sequence/combination | Infopaq International paragraphs 45-47 |
| Sweat of the brow excluded | Football Dataco paragraph 33 |
| Technical function excludes originality | BSA paragraphs 48-50 |
| Idea/expression dichotomy | SAS Institute paragraph 67 |

Operationally valuable as a **one-judgment anchor** for the entire two-step analysis of textual works.

### 3. The three cumulative criteria for idea/expression merger (paragraph 24)

For functional texts three cumulative criteria apply to establish merger:

| Criterion | Operational meaning |
|---|---|
| **Purely informative character** | The text serves only as information carrier — no literary, narrative, persuasive or analytical function |
| **Content entirely determined by information** | No design scope alongside the factual content |
| **Purely technical function** | The text serves a specific operational purpose (administrative, military, technical) |

Where cumulatively satisfied: idea and expression coincide → no work.

### 4. What the three criteria do NOT say

The judgment does **not** categorically exclude administrative texts from protection. Paragraph 25 is express: **per-case assessment** by the national court. Protection or non-protection depends on the **concrete form** of the specific text:

- Scientific reports with argumentative structure → possibly a work
- Policy notes with structure and argumentation → possibly a work
- Legal opinions with reasoning → possibly a work
- Standard forms / status reports / numerical overviews → usually no work

### 5. Operational consequence

Funke Medien provides express EU grounds for excluding protection of:

- **Standard forms** and administrative overviews
- **Status reports** with fixed structure
- **Purely factual reports** (such as meeting minutes that only record facts)
- **Technical specifications** whose content is determined by technical requirements
- **Statistical overviews and numerical overviews**
- **Press releases with predominantly factual content** (note: opinion pieces are not)

### 6. What falls outside the scope of this skill

Funke Medien further addresses:

- **Full harmonisation** of art. 2(a) and 3(1) Directive 2001/29 (paragraphs 30-38): the EU definitions of reproduction and communication right leave **no national policy margin** for Member States
- **Partial harmonisation** of art. 5(3)(c) and (d) (paragraphs 39-54): policy margin in transposing exceptions, but framed by the proportionality principle, three-step test, and Charter fundamental rights
- **No extra-directive exceptions** (paragraphs 55-64): Member States may not create additional exceptions based on fundamental rights on top of the exhaustive list of art. 5
- **Balancing of interests** in applying exceptions (paragraphs 65-76): national court weighs fundamental rights within existing exceptions

These topics belong in separate skills on copyright exceptions, communication right, and the relationship between copyright and fundamental rights.

## Argumentative consequences

### For those wishing to establish copyright protection of a functional text (pro-rightholder)

Strategy against the Funke Medien attacks:

1. **Document the creative choices in textual form** — not only what information is included, but **how** it was chosen, arranged and combined
2. **Point to alternatives** — show that the information could also have been presented in other wording, structure or arrangement
3. **Show argumentative / analytical / persuasive elements** — Funke Medien's exclusion applies only to **purely** informative texts; anyone showing that the text does more than merely communicate facts escapes the merger
4. **Request fragmentary assessment** — Funke Medien paragraph 22 expressly allows **certain fragments** to be works, even if the text as a whole is not protected

Strong examples of administrative texts that may yet be works:
- Policy notes with argumentative structure
- Legal opinions with reasoning
- Reports with conclusions and recommendations
- Research reports with analysis
- Strategic plans with vision

### For those wishing to contest copyright protection of a functional text (pro-alleged-infringer)

Attack lines from Funke Medien:

1. **Paragraph 23 — Sweat of the brow**: show that the text required intellectual effort and skill, but no free creative choices in textual form
2. **Paragraph 24 — Three cumulative criteria**: show that the text:
   - is **purely informative**
   - its **content entirely determined by information**
   - has a **purely technical function**
3. **Paragraph 24 — Idea/expression merger**: show that information and expression coincide — there was no scope for other wording
4. **Paragraph 23 — Effort does not count**: parry arguments about workload, required expertise or care in drafting — those are irrelevant
5. **Insufficient reasoning**: require the rightholder to substantiate **per text** or **per fragment** which free creative choices were made — in the absence of reasoning, seek dismissal

Strong examples of administrative texts that are usually not works:
- Standard forms and standard reports
- Statistical overviews and numerical documents
- Status reports with fixed structure (such as the UdPs themselves)
- Technical specifications
- Meeting minutes that only record facts
- Inventories and catalogues (without original selection)
- Routine administrative correspondence
