# DACH-Specific Notes

This reference gives a practical DACH overlay for Article 73 incident handling.

## 1. Germany

### Primary practical route
For Germany, treat **Bundesnetzagentur (BNetzA)** as the primary practical AI Act market-surveillance entry point for serious-incident routing under the AI Act, then verify whether a sector-specific competent authority also needs involvement.

### When to add other authorities
Add parallel analysis where the incident touches:

- **medical devices / IVD**
- **machinery / product safety**
- **critical infrastructure / cyber security**
- **employment / workplace use**
- **data protection**

### Data protection overlay
If personal data are implicated, identify the competent German **Datenschutzaufsichtsbehörde** in parallel.

### Labour / works council overlay
If the system affects workers, workplace monitoring, or personnel decisions:

- check information duties under **Article 26(7)** AI Act
- assess German **BetrVG** implications
- involve HR and, where applicable, the works council early

### Language
For Germany, prepare the report in **clear German or English** depending on channel expectations, but keep an internal German-language management summary ready for rapid escalation in Mittelstand environments.

## 2. Austria

Austria is an EU Member State, so an Article 73 report goes to the competent Austrian market-surveillance authority where the incident occurred there.

### Practical handling
- verify the current competent authority for the AI Act and affected sector
- do not delay incident qualification while recipient verification is being finalised
- prepare a recipient-neutral draft first if the facts are urgent

## 3. Switzerland

Switzerland is outside the EU AI Act’s Member State reporting structure.

### Practical implications
- no standalone Article 73 filing to a Swiss authority as an EU Member State notification
- still assess Swiss data-protection, product-safety, and contractual duties
- if the same system also caused incidents in the EU, the Swiss facts may still be relevant to EU field-corrective actions and authority communications

## 4. Mittelstand Reality Notes

For DACH companies, the biggest failure mode is not legal misunderstanding but **slow internal escalation**.

### Common blockers
- product team treats event as bug, not incident
- sales hesitates to alarm customers
- legal waits for too much certainty
- deployer information is buried in account-management channels
- no one knows who owns the authority relationship

### Better default
For plausible severe harm involving a high-risk AI system:

- appoint one incident owner
- freeze evidence fast
- make a preliminary classification memo within hours, not days
- decide reporting posture conservatively

## 5. Suggested DACH Deliverables

When answering a user from Germany/Austria/Switzerland, prefer:

- a one-page executive summary
- a clear deadline table
- a practical authority-routing note
- a customer/deployer communication script
- a management-ready action list in plain language
