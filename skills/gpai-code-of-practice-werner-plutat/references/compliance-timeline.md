> **Note on the Digital Omnibus (status as of 2026-05-08):** The European Commission proposed the Digital Omnibus simplification package in December 2025. The agreement is **not yet adopted law** — it must still be formally adopted and published in the Official Journal. **GPAI dates above are unchanged in the provisional agreement.** Only high-risk Annex III and Annex I dates would shift if formally adopted. Plan against current law.

## Obligation Phasing

### Since 2 August 2025 — All GPAI Model Providers:

| Obligation | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Technical documentation (Art. 53(1)(a)) | **IN EFFECT** | For new models placed on market |
| Downstream provider information (Art. 53(1)(b)) | **IN EFFECT** | 14-day response requirement |
| Copyright policy (Art. 53(1)(c)) | **IN EFFECT** | No open-source exemption |
| Training data summary (Art. 53(1)(d)) | **IN EFFECT** | Mandatory public disclosure |
| Cooperation with Commission/NCAs (Art. 53(3)) | **IN EFFECT** | |

### Since 2 August 2025 — Systemic Risk Providers (additionally):

| Obligation | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Model evaluation per standardised protocols (Art. 55(1)(a)) | **IN EFFECT** | Adversarial testing required |
| Systemic risk assessment and mitigation (Art. 55(1)(b)) | **IN EFFECT** | |
| Serious incident reporting (Art. 55(1)(c)) | **IN EFFECT** | 2-day deadline for critical infrastructure |
| Cybersecurity protection (Art. 55(1)(d)) | **IN EFFECT** | Model + physical infrastructure |

### Grace period — Enforcement:

| Period | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| 2 Aug 2025 – 2 Aug 2026 | Obligations apply, but AI Office works **cooperatively** with providers. No formal enforcement actions. |
| From 2 Aug 2026 | AI Office may issue: requests for information, requests for access to models, corrective measures, model recalls. |
| From 2 Aug 2026 | Fines may be imposed for non-compliance. |

**Important:** The grace period is **not a safe harbour**. Providers should be working toward compliance during this period. The Commission has stated that non-adherence to the Code of Practice will result in "a larger number of requests for information and requests for access."

### Legacy models (released before 2 August 2025):

| Deadline | Obligation |
|----------|-----------|
| 2 August 2027 | All existing GPAI models must be brought into compliance with Articles 53–55 |
| Now – Aug 2027 | Gap assessment and remediation should be underway |

## Code of Practice Lifecycle

### Current status (as of March 2026):
- Code of Practice is the **primary compliance tool** for demonstrating GPAI obligations compliance
- Code is voluntary — but non-adherence has practical consequences (increased scrutiny)
- ~Most prominent GPAI providers are signatories

### Future evolution:
- The Code will remain in effect **until harmonised European standards are adopted**
- Standardisation process for GPAI has not yet received a formal Commission request
- Standards are expected to take 2–3+ years from request to adoption (potentially 2027–2028+)
- Once standards are adopted, compliance with standards creates a **presumption of conformity**
- The Code may be revised if it proves inadequate or as the regulatory landscape evolves
- The Commission may adopt delegated acts to update FLOP thresholds, Annex XI/XII content, or other technical aspects

### Relationship between Code, standards, and compliance:

| Compliance path | Presumption of conformity | Burden of proof |
|----------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| Code of Practice signatory | Relied upon for compliance demonstration | Lower |
| Non-signatory, alternative means | Must demonstrate equivalence | Higher |
| Harmonised standards (future) | Yes — formal presumption | Lowest |

## DACH-Specific Timeline Considerations

### Germany:
- **BNetzA** (Bundesnetzagentur) designated as market surveillance authority for GPAI
- Expect BNetzA guidance and enforcement priorities to be published during 2026
- German companies with GPAI models should proactively engage with BNetzA

### Austria:
- Market surveillance authority designation in progress
- RTR (Rundfunk und Telekom Regulierungs-GmbH) likely to play a role

### Switzerland:
- Not directly subject to AI Act enforcement
- Swiss companies placing GPAI models on the EU market must comply — enforcement through EU channels
- FINMA may issue supplementary guidance for financial sector AI models
