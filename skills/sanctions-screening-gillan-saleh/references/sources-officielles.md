# Sources Officielles — Listes de Sanctions Internationales

## How to use this file
Read this file during Step 2 of the screening workflow. Use the URLs and search strategies provided to conduct real-time web searches. Always prioritize direct official source URLs over third-party aggregators.

---

## TIER 1 — Universal / Major Multilateral

### 1. United Nations — Consolidated Sanctions List
- **Authority**: UN Security Council (CSNU)
- **Official URL**: https://www.un.org/securitycouncil/content/un-sc-consolidated-list
- **Search tool**: https://scsanctions.un.org/search/
- **Coverage**: Individuals and entities listed under CSNU resolutions (Al-Qaeda, ISIL/Da'esh, Taliban, North Korea, Mali, DRC, Sudan, Somalia, Guinea-Bissau, CAR, Yemen, South Sudan, Libya, Iran, Iraq)
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" site:scsanctions.un.org` OR direct search on the UN search tool
- **Update frequency**: Near real-time (updated after each CSNU committee decision)
- **Note**: This list is the PRIMARY source — EU and national lists are largely derived from it

### 2. European Union — EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List
- **Authority**: European External Action Service (EEAS) / Council of the EU
- **Official URL**: https://www.eeas.europa.eu/eeas/european-union-sanctions_en
- **Search tool**: https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/fsd/fsf#!/files (EU Financial Sanctions Files — downloadable XML/CSV)
- **Alternative search**: https://sanctionsmap.eu (interactive map by regime)
- **Coverage**: All persons/entities sanctioned under EU PESC decisions and CFSP regulations — includes UN-derived + EU autonomous sanctions (Russia, Belarus, Iran, Syria, Myanmar, Venezuela, Nicaragua, China/Xinjiang, etc.)
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" EU financial sanctions consolidated list` OR `"[name]" site:eeas.europa.eu`
- **Update frequency**: Updated upon each Council implementing regulation (Official Journal EU)
- **Key regulations to know**: 
  - Russia: Reg. 269/2014 (asset freeze), Reg. 833/2014 (sectoral)
  - Belarus: Reg. 765/2006
  - Iran: Reg. 267/2012
  - Syria: Reg. 36/2012
  - Myanmar: Reg. 401/2013

### 3. United States — OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control)
- **Authority**: US Department of the Treasury
- **SDN List URL**: https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/financial-sanctions/specially-designated-nationals-and-blocked-persons-list-sdn-human-readable-lists
- **Search tool**: https://sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov/
- **Coverage**: 
  - SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) — full blocking
  - SSI (Sectoral Sanctions Identifications) — Russia/Ukraine sectoral
  - NS-ISA (Non-SDN Iran Sanctions Act)
  - CAPTA List (Correspondent Account/Payable-Through Account)
  - EO 13959 (Chinese Military-Industrial Complex Companies)
- **Sanctions programs**: IRAN, RUSSIA/UKRAINE, DPRK (North Korea), CUBA, VENEZUELA, SYRIA, MYANMAR, BELARUS, AFGHANISTAN, BALKANS, CYBER, FOREIGN NARCOTICS, GLOBAL MAGNITSKY, GLOMAG, SDGT (terrorism), and many others
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" OFAC SDN list` OR `"[name]" site:ofac.treas.gov` OR direct search on OFAC search tool
- **CRITICAL**: OFAC applies to "US persons" broadly — includes USD transactions, US correspondent banking
- **50% Rule**: An entity owned 50%+ by an SDN is AUTOMATICALLY treated as blocked even if not listed

### 4. United Kingdom — OFSI (Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation)
- **Authority**: HM Treasury
- **Official URL**: https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/financial-sanctions-regime-specific-legislation
- **Search tool**: https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/ (consolidated list downloadable)
- **Online search**: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/the-uk-sanctions-list
- **Coverage**: Post-Brexit autonomous UK sanctions — mirrors many EU measures but with differences; also Global Human Rights (Magnitsky-equivalent), DPRK, Russia, Belarus, Iran, Syria, Myanmar, and others
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" OFSI UK financial sanctions` OR `"[name]" site:gov.uk OFSI`
- **Update frequency**: Updated by statutory instruments (SI)

### 5. France — Direction Générale du Trésor (DGT) / DGTP
- **Authority**: Ministère de l'Économie et des Finances — Direction Générale du Trésor
- **Official URL**: https://www.tresor.economie.gouv.fr/services-aux-entreprises/sanctions-economiques/
- **Gel des avoirs list**: https://gels-avoirs.dgtresor.gouv.fr/
- **Coverage**: 
  - EU sanctions implemented in France (directly applicable via EU regulations)
  - French autonomous national sanctions (less common — e.g., some Sahel-related measures)
  - DGTRESOR issues guidance on implementation
- **Legal basis**: Code monétaire et financier, articles L. 562-1 à L. 562-10
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" site:tresor.economie.gouv.fr gel des avoirs` OR `"[name]" gel des avoirs France`
- **Note**: For France, EU list is the operative instrument — DGT provides implementation guidance

## TIER 2 — Major National Jurisdictions

### 6. Switzerland — SECO (Secrétariat d'État à l'économie)
- **Authority**: SECO — Contrôle des exportations et sanctions
- **Official URL**: https://www.seco.admin.ch/seco/fr/home/Aussenwirtschaftspolitik_Wirtschaftliche_Zusammenarbeit/Wirtschaftsbeziehungen/exportkontrollen-und-sanktionen/sanktionen-embargos.html
- **Search tool**: https://www.sesam.search.admin.ch/ (Swiss sanctions search)
- **Coverage**: CH autonomous sanctions + UN-derived; notable for: Russia, Belarus, Iran, Syria, DPRK, Myanmar, and others. Switzerland has aligned substantially with EU Russia sanctions.
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" SECO sanctions Switzerland` OR `"[name]" site:seco.admin.ch sanctions`

### 7. Canada — Global Affairs Canada / OSFI
- **Authority**: Global Affairs Canada (autonomous sanctions) + OSFI (financial implementation)
- **Official URL**: https://www.international.gc.ca/world-monde/international_relations-relations_internationales/sanctions/current-actuelles.aspx?lang=eng
- **Consolidated list**: https://www.publicsafety.gc.ca/cnt/ntnl-scrt/cntr-trrrsm/lstd-ntts/crrnt-lstd-ntts-en.aspx
- **Coverage**: Russia, Belarus, Iran, Myanmar, Venezuela, Haiti, Nicaragua, and others; also Criminal Code listed terrorist entities
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" Canada sanctions Global Affairs` OR `"[name]" site:international.gc.ca sanctions`

### 8. Australia — DFAT (Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade)
- **Authority**: DFAT — Autonomous Sanctions
- **Official URL**: https://www.dfat.gov.au/international-relations/security/sanctions
- **Consolidated list**: https://www.dfat.gov.au/sites/default/files/regulation8_consolidated.pdf
- **Coverage**: Russia, Belarus, Iran, Syria, Myanmar, DPRK, and others
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" Australia DFAT autonomous sanctions consolidated list`

### 9. Japan — METI / MOFA
- **Authority**: Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) + Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA)
- **Official URL**: https://www.meti.go.jp/policy/anpo/englishpage.html
- **Coverage**: Russia, Belarus, Iran, DPRK — Japan has expanded sanctions significantly since 2022
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" Japan METI MOFA sanctions` OR `"[name]" Japan asset freeze`

### 10. Singapore — MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore)
- **Authority**: MAS
- **Official URL**: https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/anti-money-laundering/targeted-financial-sanctions/lists-of-designated-individuals-and-entities
- **Coverage**: UN-derived + MAS-specific (DPRK, Iran, terrorism)
- **Search strategy**: `"[name]" MAS Singapore targeted financial sanctions`

## TIER 3 — Regional and Thematic

### 11. FATF — Grey and Black Lists (High-Risk Jurisdictions)
- **Official URL**: https://www.fatf-gafi.org/en/topics/high-risk-and-other-monitored-jurisdictions.html
- **Note**: Not a sanctions list per se, but directly relevant — "black list" (DPRK, Iran, Myanmar) triggers enhanced due diligence and near-total transaction restrictions in EU/FR
- **Black list (call for action)**: DPRK, Iran, Myanmar (as of 2024-2025 — verify current status)
- **Grey list**: Multiple jurisdictions subject to enhanced monitoring

### 12. EU Terrorist List (CFSP)
- **Basis**: EU Common Position 2001/931/CFSP + Council Decision 2018/1084
- **URL**: https://eeas.europa.eu/topics/sanctions-policy/8442/consolidated-list-sanctions_en
- **Note**: Separate from main CFSP sanctions — specifically targets terrorist organizations and individuals

### 13. Interpol Red Notices (not sanctions but relevant)
- **URL**: https://www.interpol.int/How-we-work/Notices/Red-Notices
- **Note**: Not a sanctions list but often correlated — check if subject appears in public Interpol notices

## Contrôle croisé systématique — TIER 2b (obligatoire)

### OpenSanctions
- **URL** : https://www.opensanctions.org/
- **Search** : `"[name]" site:opensanctions.org`
- **Couverture** : 100+ listes officielles agrégées (ONU, UE, OFAC, OFSI, SECO, Canada, Australie, Japon, Singapour, et nombreux régimes nationaux supplémentaires)
- **Rôle** : filet de sécurité — à exécuter systématiquement après le Tier 1 pour détecter des hits sur des listes non couvertes directement
- **Règle absolue** : un hit OpenSanctions doit toujours être remonté à la source officielle identifiée. Ne jamais citer OpenSanctions comme source autoritative.
- **Logique de réconciliation** :
  - Hit OpenSanctions non détecté en Tier 1 → identifier la liste source → recherche ciblée sur cette source officielle
  - OpenSanctions confirme Tier 1 → renforce la fiabilité du résultat
  - Tier 1 a un hit mais pas OpenSanctions → Tier 1 prévaut (délai de mise à jour possible)
- **Usage** : open source, gratuit pour usage non commercial

### Autres agrégateurs commerciaux (non accessibles en recherche publique)
- World-Check / Refinitiv, Dow Jones Risk & Compliance, ComplyAdvantage — outils professionnels payants, non utilisables dans ce skill

## Search Tips for Ambiguous Names

When dealing with common names or transcription variants:

1. **Try multiple transliterations**: Arabic, Russian, Chinese names have variant spellings (e.g., "Qadhafi" / "Gaddafi" / "Kadhafi")
2. **Search with date of birth** if known: `"[name]" "[DOB]" sanctions`
3. **Search with nationality/country**: `"[name]" [country] sanctions`
4. **Use OpenSanctions first** for broad multi-list search, then verify on official source
5. **For Russian/post-Soviet names**: check patronymic variants and female/male surname endings (-ov vs -ova, etc.)
6. **Flag as INCONCLUSIVE** if name match cannot be confirmed with at least 2 identifiers
